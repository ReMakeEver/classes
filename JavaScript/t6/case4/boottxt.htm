Where the Famous - and Not So Famous - Rest

Boothill was opened in 1878 and closed in 1884 when the "new" cemetery was established. In those 7 seven short years both the good citizens of Tombstone and the rascals found their eternal rest in Boothill. Here you will find the remains of such as Tom Waters (shot over the color of his shirt), Billy Clanton, Tom McLaury, Frank McLaury (shot at the OK Corral), Margarita (a saloon girl stabbed over a man), John Heath (lynched), and Hilly Hicksen (a boy who fell from a pair of stilts and died of his injury). At least the view is awesome.

We hope you enjoy your stroll with the people who made the history of Tombstone.
